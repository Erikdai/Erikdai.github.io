  1. ICML 2026
    From Knowledge to Inference: Scaling Laws of Specialized Reasoning on GlobalHealthAtlas
    Zhaokun Yan, Shan Xu, Wuzheng Dong, and 10 more authors
    In International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2026
    Abs arXiv

    GlobalHealthAtlas is a large-scale multilingual dataset (280,210 instances across 15 public-health domains and 17 languages) built with an LLM-assisted construction and quality-control pipeline. We study scaling laws of specialized public-health reasoning and propose a domain-aligned evaluator that scores outputs along six dimensions.

  2. ICML 2026 AI4Math
    The Signal-Coverage Matrix: Stratifying Type and Semantic Errors in Statement Autoformalization
    Chengxiao Dai, Zhaokun Yan, and Zhanhui Lin
    In AI for Math Workshop at the International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2026
    Abs arXiv

    A matrix-based evaluation framework for LLM autoformalization that stratifies outputs by type-correctness and semantic equivalence, revealing which error categories different methods actually address across ProofNet# and MiniF2F-test.

  3. ICLR 2026
    CLAUSE: Agentic Neuro-Symbolic Knowledge Graph Reasoning via Dynamic Learnable Context Engineering
    Yang Zhao, Chengxiao Dai, Wei Zhuo, and 2 more authors
    In International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR), 2026
    Abs arXiv

    CLAUSE is a three-agent neuro-symbolic framework that treats knowledge-graph context construction as a budgeted sequential decision process. Using the proposed LC-MAPPO (Lagrangian-Constrained Multi-Agent PPO), a Subgraph Architect, Path Navigator and Context Curator are jointly optimized under per-query budgets on edge edits, interaction steps and selected tokens.

  1. IJCNN 2025
    From Metaphor to Mechanism: How LLMs Decode Traditional Chinese Medicine Symbolic Language for Modern Clinical Relevance
    Jiacheng Tang, Nankai Wu, Fan Gao, and 3 more authors
    In International Joint Conference on Neural Networks (IJCNN), 2025
    Abs arXiv

    Metaphorical expressions abundant in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) are difficult to map onto the anatomy-driven concepts of Western Medicine (WM). We propose a multi-agent, chain-of-thought framework that interprets TCM metaphors and maps them to WM pathophysiology, combining domain-specialized agents (a TCM Expert and a WM Expert) with a Coordinator Agent and stepwise reasoning for transparency and conflict resolution.
